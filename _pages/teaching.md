My teaching emphasizes active learning, cooperative learning, scientific thinking, and inclusive classrooms. I want students to move beyond memorizing textbook material and toward understanding how biological knowledge is generated, tested, debated, and revised.

I have taught evolutionary biology, general biology, and botany lab as a discussion instructor, lab instructor, and teaching assistant in Taiwan and the United States. In these settings, I have led paper discussions, designed lab activities, supported large lecture courses, held office hours and review sessions, and helped students develop confidence in interpreting data and scientific arguments.

As a mentor, I adapt expectations to each student's stage while emphasizing research literacy, clear communication, experimental design, quantitative reasoning, and independence. I have mentored 17 undergraduate, graduate, and intern researchers, including students who contributed to peer-reviewed publications and independent research projects.
