This advanced course expands conservation genetics beyond a broad biodiversity survey. It is designed for students who want to understand the genetic mechanisms underlying population persistence, extinction risk, restoration, and conservation decision-making.

Students learn to connect foundational population genetics with modern genomic tools, including reduced-representation sequencing, whole-genome resequencing, environmental DNA, landscape genomics, demographic inference, and genetic monitoring. The course emphasizes critical reading of primary literature and applied interpretation of genetic evidence.
