People, Plants, and Food is an introductory, concept-driven course for students from diverse backgrounds. It uses crop domestication and food systems to connect plant biology, human history, culture, sustainability, and biodiversity conservation.

The course asks how plants became crops, why modern food systems rely on a small subset of plant diversity, and how agriculture can be made more resilient under climate change. Students engage with case studies across staple crops, underused crops, crop wild relatives, biotechnology, and agrobiodiversity.

| Week | Theme | Core Concepts |
| --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Origin of agriculture and what is a crop? | Foraging, cultivation, farming, domestication, staple crops, diversified food systems |
| 2 | People, plants, and evolution | Heritable variation, selection, domestication as visible evolution |
| 3 | Basic biology of crop domestication I | Mating systems, clonality, polyploidy, standing variation, seed dormancy and dispersal |
| 4 | Basic biology of crop domestication II | Photoperiod, flowering, life history, plant organs of use |
| 5 | Staples and underused grains | Wheat, maize, rice, millets, sorghum, drought resilience, agrobiodiversity loss |
| 6 | Roots and tubers | Potato, cassava, taro, yams, sweet potato, clonal propagation, toxins, storage |
| 7 | Legumes | Soybean, common bean, adzuki bean, cowpea, lupin, nitrogen fixation, nutrition |
| 8 | Fruits | Banana, apple, guava, breadfruit, shelf life, clonality, trade, consumer preference |
| 9 | Vegetables and leafy greens | Tomato, Brassicas, amaranth greens, water spinach, nutrition, market traits |
| 10 | Stimulants, spices, and sugar | Sugarcane, coffee, tea, cacao, vanilla, pepper, chili, secondary metabolites |
| 11 | Crop wild relatives | Landraces, modern cultivars, in situ and ex situ conservation, seed banks |
| 12 | GMO and Bt crops | Resistance evolution, refuges, gene pyramids, governance |
| 13 | Herbicide tolerance and sustainability | Weed evolution, glyphosate, selection pressure, ecological tradeoffs |
| 14 | Climate change, resilience, and future crops | Heat, drought, salinity, phenology, genotype-by-environment variation |
